1. Thermo Map

Thermomap helps us to graphically see departments where cases of violence have occurred.


Nevertheless, it is possible to make comparisons, but the area of a department and its population can confuse us.

2. Barchart using the total number of cases

Alphabetically sorted

Ordering alphabetically a bar chart helps us to locate a department easily


but, what if we want to find extremes?

2. Barchart using the total number of cases

Sorted by value

If the task is to find extremes, sorting by values is the best option.


However, the total number of cases is not a comparable measure between departments.

3. A relative measure

Using a relative measure as the number of cases per 100,000 inhabitants allows us to compare departments in a better way.


As a result, there are no considerable differences.

Finding extremes

Casanare, Bogotá, Meta, Guaviare, and Arauca

The departments with the most number of cases of violence against children per 100,000 inhabitants


What about gender?

4. Stacked Barchart

Men vs Women

A stacked bar chart is ideal for summarizing since we can see the total value and genders at the same time.


However, in this case, it is not ideal to compare between genders.

5. Grouped Barchart

Men vs Women

Genders implicated in events of violence per department are more easily compared in this way.


What department has the highest percentage of women implicated in events of violence against children?

Percentage by department?

Guanía has the highest percentage of women, while San Andres has the highest percentage of men involved in events of violence against children.
